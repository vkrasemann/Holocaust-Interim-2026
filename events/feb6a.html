Breakfast at hostel - February 6th, 2026

A traditional German breakfast is hearty, simple, and satisfying — perfect fuel for a busy day.

It usually starts with a selection of fresh bread rolls (Brötchen), such as crusty white rolls or rye-based varieties. These are served with a variety of toppings, including:

Cold cuts (Wurst & Schinken) – like ham, salami, or liverwurst
Cheeses – mild cheeses like Gouda or Emmental
Butter and jams – homemade fruit preserves or honey
Boiled eggs, often served soft- or medium-cooked
Fresh vegetables – like sliced cucumbers, tomatoes, or radishes
Yogurt or muesli – with fresh fruit and nuts, especially in youth hostels
Coffee or tea – strong and served hot, sometimes with hot cocoa for kids
Juice – usually orange or apple

In youth hostels, it’s common to find all of this laid out buffet-style.
