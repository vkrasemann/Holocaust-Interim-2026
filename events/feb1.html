Holocaust Interim – February 2026

Dear Parents and Students,

We are excited to share more details about our upcoming interim trip to Munich, Berlin, and Krakow (Auschwitz) in February 2026. This trip will provide a powerful and meaningful opportunity for students to deepen their understanding of the Holocaust through history, culture, and first-hand experience.

Cost

  • Estimated cost (excluding flights): $1,300 per person
  • Part of this amount will be in cash (about $300), which students will use for small meals (such as street food), local purchases, and incidental expenses.
  • Flights already booked separately and shared as soon as confirmed.

Documents & Money

Please make sure the following items are ready well before departure:

  • Valid passport (check expiration date – must be valid 6 months past return date).
  • Plane ticket
  • Entry ticket to Germany (will be purchased as part of travel logistics). The EU introduced ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorization System) a few months ago. This is an online pre-travel authorization — similar to the U.S. ESTA. It will cost about €7 (waived for travelers under 18) and must be completed before departure. It’s not a visa, but more like a security pre-check.
  • Debit card to draw money while in Germany and Poland. A lot of Germany still requires cash. Using a debit card is more convenient and cheaper than exchanging money at a bank in the US. If this proves to be difficult, please let us know.
  • Travel health insurance (details will be provided by the school).

Communication Abroad

We want all students to be able to text each other and their chaperones during our trip — safely and affordably.

Two Options for Cell Phone Use in Europe:

  1. Use Your U.S. Carrier’s Travel Plan
    • Most carriers (Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile) charge about $12/day.
    • Convenient but can cost nearly $170 for two weeks.
  2. Use a Prepaid Travel eSIM (Recommended if your phone is unlocked)
    • A digital SIM card you download onto your phone.
    • Cost: about $25–$40 for 7–15 days of data in Germany and Poland.
    • Providers include: Airalo, Holafly, Orange Holiday Europe, Nomad, Ubigi.
    • Works perfectly for texting apps (iMessage, WhatsApp, Signal, Messenger).

Recommendation: We strongly encourage families to choose a prepaid eSIM. It is affordable, reliable, and prevents surprise roaming charges.

Packing List

Students should travel light but be prepared for winter weather.

Clothing

  • Rain jacket
  • Warm puffy jacket
  • Good walking shoes (running shoes are fine)
  • Warm hat, gloves, scarf (February in Germany/Poland is cold and damp)
  • Layering pieces (sweaters, long-sleeve shirts, thermal base layers)
  • Extra socks (dry feet matter when walking in winter!)
  • Small notebook/journal + pen (to capture reflections on memorials, museums, and sites)
  • Books/e-reader (for flights or quiet evenings)
  • Sleep mask/earplugs (shared hostel rooms can be noisy)

Technology

  • Portable charger
  • European plug adapter (you will need one to charge your phone)
  • If you are using Apple chargers, you can bring your power adapter and just change the plug.
  • Headphones/earbuds (for downtime, flights, or audio tours)

Optional but Recommended

  • Sleep mask/earplugs (shared hostel rooms can be noisy)

Bags

  • Checked bag: either a roller suitcase or a backpack.
  • Carry-on: must be a small backpack (not a second roller, we will use it for daily excursions).
